About

Timothy Hall is a current graduate student at Portland State University pursuing a M.S. in Computer Science with a focus on AI and machine learning. He earned a B.S. of Computer Science from Portland State. His journey down this path began back in 2013 where he self taught himself java and was instantly hooked. Years of making small games has evolved to becoming passionate about image processing and computer vision. In 2021 he lead the development of an image classifier for Sightline Applications to identify cars and people for onboard video surveillance systems. Tim plans to contribute to projects for the medical field or self driving cars in the future. In his free time he makes things and spends time in the outdoors.

Projects

Median Image Filter

This project evaluates the performance of the median filter algorithm proposed in Median Filtering in Constant Time. The performance of my implementation of the constant time algorithm is compared against a similar O(n) algorithm the constant time one was adapted from and a trivial O(n2)-ish variation. My results were conclusive even though my algorithm to calculate the mean value was not constant time but rather O(n).

Frupal Game

Frupal is a python terminal game made for a software engineering class. Players wander around an island to find gem stones using tools to overcome obsticals. The primary focus of the project was working as a team to develop a project, as well as to familiarize ourselves with the agile design process and project life cycle.
